## AMENDMENT 1 (2026-06-05) — boundary floor omitted; GO re-confirmed at corrected config L=32/N=8192/T=10

The original GO config (L=10/N=4096/T=10) was empirically FALSIFIED on bestfriend: both gates floored at err ≈ **1.36e-2** by n=4–8 (g_zeta6 4→8 = −0.66, 8→16 = −0.04; g_zeta8 4→8 = −0.04), 9–10 orders above the modelled floors. ROOT CAUSE: the global-error model `err_global = c·T·τ^K + (T·φ)/τ + C_sp·T·dx^p` **omitted the BOUNDARY error**. The test compares the finite-domain Neumann (zero-flux clamp) solution against the *infinite-line* heat kernel, whose boundary amplitude is `u(T,±L) = (1+4T)^{−½}·exp(−L²/(1+4T))` = **1.3626e-2 at L=10,T=10** — matching the observed plateau to 99.8% (`C_bdry ≈ 1.0`). This term is τ- and N-independent (a flat floor → slope→0); the engineer's `ZeroExtend` experiment gave the same constant error because *no* finite-domain BC is exact for a kernel non-zero at the boundary. The L-vs-N tension is illusory: the boundary floor decays **Gaussian** in L while the spatial floor grows only **polynomially** ((2L/N)^(p−2)), so a wide stable window exists (floors cross at L*≈32.5, both ≈9e-13). Receding the boundary into the Gaussian tail (L: 10→32) crushes the boundary floor to 2.2e-12 while keeping the 6th-order spatial floor at 1.4e-11, both ≥4 orders below the temporal signal at every ladder point. The corrected oracle `scripts/verify_zeta_truthful_order_octonic_v2.py` (a) reproduces the 1.36e-2 plateau, (b) reproduces the L=10 collapse, (c) finds 28 feasible configs at N≤16384, (d) confirms **GO at L=32/N=8192/T=10/p=6** with both middle-pair slopes = −K to 4 d.p. and PASS under ±1000× floor-constant perturbation. **CORRECTED ENGINEER SPEC**: change `X_MIN/X_MAX` from ∓10 to **∓32** and keep N=8192 (NOT 4096 — dx must stay ≤7.8e-3) in both `tests/zeta{6,8}_truthful_order.rs`; keep IC `exp(−x²)`, T=10, ladder {2,4,8,16}, oracle `(1+4T)^{−½}·exp(−x²/(1+4T))`, Neumann BC unchanged (correct once L is large; do NOT switch to ZeroExtend). The OCTONIC sampler (ADR-0117) and 6th-order stencil (ADR-0118) SHIP unchanged as accepted building blocks. Margin-optimal alternative: L=35/N=16384 (2.4e4× margin).
