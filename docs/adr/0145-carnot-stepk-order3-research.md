# ADR-0145 — General step-k order≥3 Carnot closure (research register C-8)

**Status:** PROPOSED — **PARTIAL CLOSE** (specific-family GO on record; general-k convergence theorem stays OPEN/escalated) · **Date:** 2026-06-08 · **Branch:** `feat/v8.0.0-planning`
**Wave-3 register:** C-8 (Festschrift §3 OPEN problem — constructive hypoelliptic order≥3 product formula for general step-k Carnot groups)
**Pre-flight:** `scripts/carnot_stepk_order3_preflight.py` — VERDICT: **GO (partial close)** · **Parents:** ADR-0136 (Amendments 1 & 2), ADR-0077, ADR-0095

## Context

The Festschrift §3 problem asks for a *constructive* hypoelliptic product formula of order ≥ 3 for **general step-k** Carnot groups. Prior work split the question in two. ADR-0136 **Amendment 1** proved (two independent sympy witnesses) that the *real-coefficient* "palindrome-of-palindromes" route is order **2 only** on any Carnot step: the τ³ coefficient of `log Ψ` is the fixed non-zero combo `−1/24[A,[A,B]] + 1/12[B,[B,A]]`, uncancellable by any palindromic wrap of the two horizontal legs. ADR-0136 **Amendment 2** opened the *complex-time* escape (Sheng–Suzuki barrier circumvented on the analytic hypoelliptic semigroup) and proved order **4** via the complex triple-jump `Ψ(τ)=S(γ⋆τ)∘S((1−2γ⋆)τ)∘S(γ⋆τ)`, but with **one** concrete operator witness only — filiform N=5 (step 4). C-8 asks the residual open question and tries to **falsify** it: is the order-4 cancellation genuinely **step-independent**, or an artifact of that single group? And what part of "general-k" is actually shippable vs. honestly open?

## Decision

**PARTIAL CLOSE.** Record a constructive **specific-family** order-4 result and re-affirm the general-k *convergence theorem* as OPEN/escalated. No new Rust code or gate is mandated by this register; the deliverable is the sympy oracle + this finding. Two facts decide it:

1. **The order conditions are step-independent scalar equations** (re-derived in preflight W0). The triple-jump order-3 condition is the scalar cubic `2γ³+(1−2γ)³=0`; its admissible complex root `γ⋆ = 0.32439640402017117 ∓ 0.1345862724908067 i` has `Re(γ⋆)=0.324>0` **and** `Re(1−2γ⋆)=0.351>0` (every sub-map forward-in-time/bounded); the τ⁴ term vanishes by the even-order theorem (symmetric method). The brackets `[A,[A,B]],[B,[B,A]]` enter only as **abstract free-Lie words** — the Carnot step never appears in the order conditions. It governs hypoellipticity (whether `e^{τL}` is analytic/smoothing), not the splitting order.

2. **A second, structurally distinct operator witness confirms step-independence is real, not a one-group accident** (preflight W1). The complex triple-jump reaches order 4 on the **Engel group** (filiform N=4, step **3** — distinct from the shipped filiform-N5 step-4 witness), measured EXACTLY on a generic degree-10 non-origin jet, with a real-Strang order-2 control on the identical probe certifying the probe is non-degenerate (per the ADR-0136 Amendment-1 honesty note: a low-degree/origin-symmetric probe over-reports the order because `L^k` lowers polynomial degree by 2). The same `γ⋆` works on both groups (W2), the honest meaning of "step does not enter the order."

**Shippable vs. open boundary.** *Constructive* order-4 splitting is available for any step-k Carnot group that ships an order-2 symmetric horizontal base `S` (the construction is `ComplexTripleJump<S>`, already in v8.0.0 for filiform-N5; instantiating it for another group needs only that group's order-2 `S`). What stays OPEN is the **general-k strong-operator convergence proof**: it rides the analytic-semigroup complex-time tangency framework (Castella–Chartier–Descombes–Vilmart 2009 / Hansen–Ostermann 2009 / Galkin–Remizov 2025 *IJM*) as a **CITATION**, not a sympy-provable identity. The K-tangency hypothesis (a Taylor-remainder bound on `S(t)f − Σ tᵏLᵏf/k!`) is operator-algebraic and step-agnostic, so extension is *plausible* but *unproven* — identical conditional status to Theorem 28.bis (order-2 Engel). This is the honest residue, escalated (mirrors the G_zeta4 / ADR-0075 precedent).

## TRIZ analysis (ARIZ chain)

- **АП (НЭ):** the τ³ commutator term survives every palindromic wrap of the two horizontal legs ⇒ general-k order is stuck at 2; a constructive order≥3 is needed but the obvious symmetry route is exhausted.
- **ТП:** *(inherited/confirmed from ADR-0136 Amdt 2)* a real splitting of order ≥3 must contain a negative substep (Sheng–Suzuki) ⇒ runs the hypoelliptic semigroup backward-in-time, unbounded/ill-posed; but an all-forward (bounded) real splitting is capped at order 2.
- **ФП:** the substep duration `c` must be simultaneously **forward/bounded** (`Re(c)>0`, so the sectorial `e^{−cτL}` stays bounded) **and** **order-raising** (carry the cancellation a negative real step would supply).
- **ИКР:** the substep itself, using a resource already in the system, supplies the τ³/τ⁴ cancellation while every factor stays a bounded forward semigroup value — no extra legs, no backward step.
- **Разрешение (надсистема → расширение поля коэффициентов до ℂ; разрешение в структуре комплексного числа):** split `c = Re(c) + i·Im(c)` with `Re(c)>0` (bounded) and `Im(c)≠0` (cancellation). The decisive ВПР is **already shipped**: `SemiflowComplex` + complex Cayley + `GridFnND<C>`. C-8's specific TRIZ contribution beyond Amdt 2 is the **step-independence resolution**: the contradiction "the construction is verified on one group but the claim is general-k" is resolved by **separation in structure** — split the claim into the *constructive* part (step-independent scalar order conditions, witnessed on two distinct step groups: Engel step-3 here + filiform-N5 step-4 prior) which is GO, and the *convergence-theorem* part (analytic-tangency framework) which stays a citation/OPEN. Do not paper over the gap with a "general-k proved" claim — that would be the golden-middle compromise the method forbids.

## Findings

- **Literature:** the complex-coefficient escape of the order-2 barrier for analytic semigroups is established (CCDV 2009 *BIT*; Hansen–Ostermann 2009 *BIT*; Sheng 1989 / Goldman–Kaper 1996 for the barrier itself). Order conditions are coefficient (scalar) equations — the Suzuki–Yoshida triple/quadruple-jump reaches orders 3–14, ceiling 14. **No published source** constructs an order≥3 *hypoelliptic/Carnot* product formula for *general* step-k with a convergence proof; the gap is the analytic-tangency extension, not the algebra.
- **Sympy VERDICT (`carnot_stepk_order3_preflight.py`):** **GO (partial close).** W0 (free-algebra order conditions) all PASS: the admissible complex root is `γ⋆ = 0.32439640402017117 ∓ 0.1345862724908067 i` (full-precision, matches the shipped `GAMMA_STAR` in `carnot_complex.rs` exactly), `Re(γ⋆)=0.324>0` and `Re(1−2γ⋆)=0.351>0`, τ²/τ³/τ⁴ all cancel at `γ⋆`. W1 (Engel step-3, N=4) PASS: real-Strang control first mismatch at τ³ (order 2, probe certified non-degenerate); complex triple-jump first mismatch at **τ⁵** ⇒ global order **4** on a genuine step-3 group — a SECOND operator witness distinct from the shipped filiform-N5 step-4 one. W2 PASS: the same `γ⋆` is the exact algebraic root and matches the shipped `GAMMA_STAR` constant (full-precision, correct in source and all docs). **No surviving bracket term — the operator side is fully GO; the only OPEN residue is the convergence-theorem framework extension.**
- **Recommendation:** keep the v8.0.0 `ComplexTripleJump` order-4 result as the headline; record Engel step-3 as a SECOND verified family (math on record, no new gate required); re-affirm the **general-k convergence theorem as OPEN/escalated**, the obstruction localised to the analytic-tangency framework extension (the order-condition algebra is closed and step-independent). Reopenable to a full GO only by a proof (or citation) that the Galkin–Remizov tangency framework admits complex-time substeps on general step-k Carnot generators.

## Consequences

Closes the C-8 register honestly as a partial close: the constructive order-4 splitting is now witnessed on **two** distinct step groups (step-3 Engel, step-4 filiform-N5), strengthening the v8.0.0 headline claim against a single-group-accident objection; and the precise remaining open kernel is localised to a citeable framework extension rather than left as a vague "general-k is open." Zero new code/deps/gates from this register; the order-2 real baseline (`carnot_stepk.rs`) and order-4 complex kernel (`carnot_complex.rs`) already shipped under ADR-0136 are unaffected.
