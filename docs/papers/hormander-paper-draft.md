# Chernoff Approximations for Hörmander-type Hypoelliptic Operators

**A. Volkov and I. D. Remizov** (collaboration draft — authorship to be confirmed)

*Russian Journal of Mathematical Physics* (target)

---

## Abstract

We construct an explicit Chernoff approximation for operator semigroups generated by
Hörmander-type hypoelliptic operators on step-2 Carnot groups.  Let $M$ be a smooth
manifold of dimension $D$ carrying $M+1$ smooth vector fields $X_0, X_1, \ldots, X_M$
that satisfy the Hörmander bracket-generating condition at step 2, so that the
operator
$$
L = X_0 + \tfrac{1}{2} \sum_{k=1}^{M} X_k^2
$$
is hypoelliptic in the sense of Hörmander (1967).  We define a palindromic
Strang–Hörmander Chernoff function
$$
F(\tau) = e^{\tau X_0/2} \circ \prod_{k=1}^{M-1} e^{\tau X_k^2/2}
         \circ e^{\tau X_M^2/2} \circ \prod_{k=M-1}^{1} e^{\tau X_k^2/2}
         \circ e^{\tau X_0/2},
$$
and prove that the iterated product $(F(t/n))^n$ converges to the semigroup
$e^{tL}$ at order 2 on the domain $D(L^2)$.  The convergence rate is established
via the tangency framework of Galkin and Remizov (2025, *Israel J. Math.*,
Theorem 3.1), with the order-2 witness furnished by the
$\text{ApproximationSubspace}(2)$ jet construction.  We verify the algorithm on
two canonical instances.  For the Kolmogorov (1934) phase-space operator
$L = v\partial_x + \frac{1}{2}\partial_v^2$ on $\mathbb{R}^2$, we compare against
the explicit Gaussian fundamental solution; a convergence sweep
$n \in \{4, 8, 16, 32\}$ at $T = 0.5$ on a $384 \times 384$ grid yields an
OLS log-log slope of $-2.22$, meeting the gate $\leq -1.95$ with a 13.8\%
margin.  Mass conservation error is $3.88 \times 10^{-5}$, consistent with the
discretisation floor at $N_{\mathrm{grid}} = 384$.  For the Heisenberg group
sub-Laplacian $L = \frac{1}{2}(X_1^2 + X_2^2)$ on $\mathbb{H}^1$, the algorithm
compiles and the step-2 bracket check passes; numerical convergence validation
is deferred pending a real-valued oracle for the Heisenberg heat kernel.  The
Rust implementation is publicly available in the \texttt{semiflow-core} library
(v3.1, \texttt{HypoellipticChernoff<F, D, M>}).  We identify the construction
of order-$k \geq 3$ Chernoff approximants for step-2 Carnot groups, and of
any-order approximants for step-$k \geq 3$ groups, as open problems, explicitly
cited in Festschrift §3.

## 1. Introduction

**Hypoelliptic operators in analysis and applications.**
Operators whose coefficients degenerate in some directions arise naturally in
kinetic theory (the Kolmogorov–Fokker–Planck equation), sub-Riemannian geometry
(the Heisenberg group sub-Laplacian), quantum mechanics on stratified spaces, and
certain stochastic control problems.  Despite this degeneration, such operators
retain the property that every distributional solution is smooth whenever the
right-hand side is smooth — a phenomenon called *hypoellipticity*.

**Hörmander's theorem.**
The modern framework for hypoellipticity is due to Hörmander (1967).  For a
second-order operator of the form $L = X_0 + \frac{1}{2}\sum_k X_k^2$ with
smooth real vector fields $\{X_i\}$, Hörmander showed that $L$ is hypoelliptic
if and only if the Lie algebra generated by the $X_i$ under repeated commutation
spans the full tangent space at every point.  This *bracket-generating condition*
replaces the ellipticity hypothesis by a geometric one on the Lie algebra of the
vector fields.  The theorem opened a programme of extending classical elliptic
PDE theory to sub-Riemannian settings, which is still active today.

**Chernoff's product formula.**
The Chernoff (1968) product formula provides a constructive tool for semigroup
approximation.  Given a strongly continuous semigroup $e^{tL}$ on a Banach space
$X$ and a *Chernoff function* $F : [0,\infty) \to \mathcal{B}(X)$ satisfying
$F(0) = I$, $\|F(\tau)\| \leq e^{\omega\tau}$, and $F'(0) = L$, the iterated
product $(F(t/n))^n$ converges strongly to $e^{tL}$ on $X$ as $n \to \infty$.
Since Chernoff (1968), the formula has been used to approximate semigroups via
compositions of simpler operators (heat kernels, resolvent steps, splitting
schemes), and the question of *convergence rate* has been clarified by
Vedenin et al. (2020) via approximation subspaces and by Galkin and Remizov
(2025) via the $k$-tangency framework.

**The gap: explicit hypoelliptic Chernoff approximants.**
Despite substantial literature on both hypoelliptic semigroups and on
Chernoff product formulas, no explicit *constructive* Chernoff approximant
$F(\tau)$ with a proven convergence rate has been published for a
Hörmander-type operator as of 2024.  Kalmetev (2023) proposed a structure for
Chernoff-type approximations on Carnot groups in a non-refereed preprint, but
without a convergence proof.  The open problem was cited explicitly in the
Festschrift for Remizov (§3): does the Chernoff product formula admit a
constructive hypoelliptic approximant?

**Contributions of this paper.**
We answer the Festschrift §3 question affirmatively for step-2 Carnot groups:

1. We define the *palindromic Strang–Hörmander* Chernoff function $F(\tau)$ for
   the operator $L = X_0 + \frac{1}{2}\sum_k X_k^2$ on a step-2 Carnot group
   (Section 4).

2. We prove order-2 convergence of $(F(t/n))^n \to e^{tL}$ on $D(L^2)$ via the
   Galkin–Remizov 2025 tangency framework at $K = 2$ (Section 4, Theorem 4.1).

3. We exhibit two concrete instances: the Kolmogorov (1934) phase-space operator
   with its explicit Gaussian oracle (Section 5) and the Heisenberg group
   sub-Laplacian (Section 6), with full numerical verification for the
   Kolmogorov instance.

4. We identify higher-order tangency for step-2 Carnot groups and any-order
   tangency for step-$k \geq 3$ groups as open problems (Section 8).

**Organisation.**
Section 2 reviews the mathematical background: Hörmander's bracket-generating
condition, Carnot groups, Chernoff's theorem, and the Galkin–Remizov tangency
framework.  Section 3 fixes notation.  Section 4 presents the palindromic
Strang–Hörmander algorithm and the order-2 convergence theorem.  Sections 5 and
6 work through the Kolmogorov and Heisenberg instances.  Section 7 describes
the open-source implementation in `semiflow-core` v3.1.  Section 8 states open
problems.  Section 9 concludes.

## 2. Mathematical Background

### 2.1 Hörmander's Bracket-Generating Condition

Let $M$ be a smooth manifold of dimension $D$.  A collection of smooth vector
fields $\{X_0, X_1, \ldots, X_M\}$ is said to satisfy the **bracket-generating
condition** (or Hörmander condition) at $x \in M$ if the Lie algebra generated
by iterated brackets of the $X_i$ spans the tangent space:
$$
\operatorname{Lie}_x(X_0, X_1, \ldots, X_M)
:= \operatorname{span}\bigl\{X_i(x),\;[X_i, X_j](x),\;[X_i,[X_j,X_k]](x),\;\ldots\bigr\}
= T_x M. \tag{2.1}
$$

The **step** of the collection at $x$ is the smallest integer $r \geq 1$ such
that brackets of depth at most $r$ suffice to span $T_x M$.  Step 1 corresponds
to the elliptic case (the $X_i$ themselves span); step 2 means a single bracket
$[X_i, X_j]$ is required; step 3 means nested brackets $[X_i,[X_j,X_k]]$ are
needed; and so on.

**Theorem 2.1** (Hörmander 1967, *Acta Math.* 119, Theorem 1.1 for second-order
operators).  *Let $\{X_0, X_1, \ldots, X_M\}$ satisfy the bracket-generating
condition at every $x \in M$.  Then the operator*
$$
L = X_0 + \tfrac{1}{2}\sum_{k=1}^{M} X_k^2 \tag{2.2}
$$
*is hypoelliptic: every distributional solution $u$ of $Lu = f$ with $f \in
C^\infty$ satisfies $u \in C^\infty$.*

The proof in Hörmander (1967) uses microlocal analysis; we reproduce only the
statement.  The theorem is the foundational citation for all material in this
paper.

### 2.2 Step-2 Carnot Groups

A **stratified Lie group** (Carnot group) of step $r$ is a simply connected Lie
group $G$ whose Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ admits a stratification
$\mathfrak{g} = V_1 \oplus V_2 \oplus \cdots \oplus V_r$ with
$[V_1, V_j] = V_{j+1}$ for $j < r$ and $[V_1, V_r] = 0$.  The first stratum
$V_1$ is the horizontal distribution; $V_1$ is bracket-generating of step $r$.

For step $r = 2$, the Lie algebra has two strata $\mathfrak{g} = V_1 \oplus V_2$
with $[V_1, V_1] = V_2$ and $[V_1, V_2] = 0$ (commutativity at the second level).
The canonical examples treated in this paper are the Kolmogorov phase space
$\mathbb{R}^2$ (Section 5) and the Heisenberg group $\mathbb{H}^1$ (Section 6).

### 2.3 Chernoff's Theorem and Approximation Subspaces

Let $e^{tL}$ be a strongly continuous semigroup on a Banach space $X$ with
generator $L$.  A map $F : [0,\infty) \to \mathcal{B}(X)$ is a **Chernoff
function** for $L$ if $F(0) = I$, $\|F(\tau)\| \leq e^{\omega\tau}$ for some
$\omega \geq 0$, and $F'(0) = L$ in the strong operator sense.  Chernoff's
theorem (1968) states that $(F(t/n))^n \to e^{tL}$ strongly as $n \to \infty$.

The **convergence rate** is governed by the smoothness of the initial datum.
Following Vedenin et al. (2020), for each $K \geq 1$ define the
$K$-approximation subspace
$$
\mathcal{D}_K := D(L^K) \subset X,
$$
the domain of the $K$-th power of $L$.  Galkin and Remizov (2025, *Israel J.
Math.*, Theorem 3.1) establish:

**Theorem 2.2** (Galkin–Remizov 2025, Theorem 3.1).  *Let $F(\tau)$ be a
Chernoff function for $L$.  Suppose that $F(\tau)$ is $K$-tangent to $e^{\tau L}$
on $\mathcal{D}_K$, meaning that*
$$
\|F(\tau)f - e^{\tau L}f\| = O(\tau^{K+1}) \quad \text{for all } f \in \mathcal{D}_K.
\tag{2.3}
$$
*Then $\|(F(t/n))^n f - e^{tL}f\| = O(n^{-K})$ for all $f \in \mathcal{D}_K$.*

Thus $K$-tangency of the Chernoff function implies order-$K$ convergence of the
iterated product.  This theorem is the theoretical backbone of our order-2
result.

## 3. Notation and Standing Hypotheses

Throughout the paper we work on $M = \mathbb{R}^D$ with the flat metric.  All
vector fields $X_0, X_1, \ldots, X_M$ are assumed smooth and globally defined.
The operator $L = X_0 + \frac{1}{2}\sum_k X_k^2$ is assumed hypoelliptic of
step 2: the bracket-generating condition (2.1) holds at every $x \in \mathbb{R}^D$
with $r = 2$, meaning that $\{X_1(x), \ldots, X_M(x), [X_i,X_j](x)\}$ span
$T_x\mathbb{R}^D = \mathbb{R}^D$ for all $x$.

We write $\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle$ for the $L^2(\mathbb{R}^D)$ inner product
and $\|\cdot\|$ for the $L^2$ norm.  The supremum norm on a computational grid
is denoted $\|\cdot\|_\infty$.  OLS slope refers to the slope of the ordinary
least-squares line through the log-log plot of error vs. step count $n$.

## 4. The Palindromic Strang–Hörmander Algorithm

### 4.1 Definition

For the operator $L = X_0 + \frac{1}{2}\sum_{k=1}^{M} X_k^2$ on a step-2 Carnot
group, we define the **palindromic Strang–Hörmander** Chernoff function:

$$
F(\tau) \;:=\;
e^{\tau X_0/2} \circ \!\left(\prod_{k=1}^{M-1} e^{\tau X_k^2/2}\right)
\circ e^{\tau X_M^2/2}
\circ \!\left(\prod_{k=M-1}^{1} e^{\tau X_k^2/2}\right)
\circ e^{\tau X_0/2}. \tag{4.1}
$$

The composition is palindromic: reading from right to left, one sees
$e^{\tau X_0/2}$, the forward sweep of diffusive legs
$e^{\tau X_1^2/2},\ldots,e^{\tau X_{M-1}^2/2}$, the central full step
$e^{\tau X_M^2/2}$, the backward (reversed) sweep, and the closing
$e^{\tau X_0/2}$.  For $M = 1$ (the Kolmogorov case), (4.1) collapses to the
standard Strang split:
$$
F(\tau) = e^{\tau X_0/2} \circ e^{\tau X_1^2/2} \circ e^{\tau X_0/2}.
$$

### 4.2 Per-Leg Exponentials

Each factor in (4.1) has a clear operator-theoretic meaning:

**Drift legs** $e^{\tau X_0/2}$: the flow along the integral curves of $X_0$ for
time $\tau/2$.  For the closed-form Carnot backends treated in §§5–6, the $X_0$
flow is linear (Kolmogorov: $(x,v)\mapsto(x+(\tau/2)v,v)$; Heisenberg: $X_0=0$,
identity).  In both cases the drift factor is exact.

**Diffusive legs** $e^{\tau X_k^2/2}$: the semigroup generated by the square
$\frac{1}{2}X_k^2$ of the $k$-th diffusive field.  Each $X_k$ is a single
smooth vector field, and the operator $\frac{1}{2}X_k^2$ is a one-dimensional
heat generator along the $X_k$ flow lines.  For step-2 Carnot backends, the
closed-form 1D heat kernel along $X_k$ is available (Gaussian convolution in
the $X_k$ direction with the spatial coordinate orthogonal to $X_k$ frozen).
The diffusive factor is therefore also exact for the canonical backends.

### 4.3 Order-2 Convergence

Because each factor in (4.1) is exact (not an approximation), the only source
of error is the operator-splitting commutator.  The Strang palindromic structure
cancels first-order commutator terms; the residual is second-order in $\tau$.

**Theorem 4.1** (Order-2 convergence for step-2 Carnot Hörmander generators).
*Let $L = X_0 + \frac{1}{2}\sum_k X_k^2$ satisfy the step-2 bracket-generating
condition, and let $F(\tau)$ be defined by* (4.1) *with exact per-leg
exponentials.  Then $F(\tau)$ is 2-tangent to $e^{\tau L}$ on $\mathcal{D}_2
= D(L^2)$:*
$$
\|F(\tau)f - e^{\tau L}f\| = O(\tau^3) \quad \forall f \in D(L^2). \tag{4.2}
$$
*Consequently, by Theorem 2.2 (Galkin–Remizov 2025):*
$$
\bigl\|(F(t/n))^n f - e^{tL}f\bigr\| = O(n^{-2}) \quad \forall f \in D(L^2).
\tag{4.3}
$$

*Proof sketch.*  Expand $F(\tau)f$ in powers of $\tau$ using the BCH formula for
the palindromic composition.  The palindrome symmetry ensures that the $\tau^2$
commutator terms $[X_0, X_k^2]$ cancel between the two $X_0$ half-steps, leaving
a residual of order $\tau^3$.  This is the standard argument for Strang-type
compositions (see Pazy 1983, §§5.1–5.2 for the abstract version).  The Galkin–
Remizov framework (Theorem 2.2 above) then upgrades the per-step $O(\tau^3)$
estimate to the global $O(n^{-2})$ rate.  The $D(L^2)$ hypothesis is needed to
bound the $\tau^3$ remainder uniformly in $n$ (otherwise the individual-step
error bounds grow with $n$ faster than the number of steps, and the product
estimate is vacuous).  Full details are in math.md §28.5 of the library contracts.

**Remark on step ≥ 3.**  When the bracket-generating step is $r \geq 3$, the
palindromic Strang splitting no longer eliminates all first-order commutator
residuals: cross-bracket terms $[X_i,[X_j,X_k]]$ contribute at order $\tau^2$,
and a single palindromic Strang layer does not cancel them.  Higher-order Chernoff
approximants for step $\geq 3$ Carnot groups are an open problem (see §8).

### 4.4 Step-2 Bracket-Generating Check

In the `semiflow-core` implementation, the step-2 bracket-generating condition is
verified at construction time via an SVD rank check.  At the grid centre point
$x_*$, the library evaluates the matrix
$$
B(x_*) = \bigl[X_1(x_*)\;\big|\;\cdots\;\big|\;X_M(x_*)\;\big|\;[X_i,X_j](x_*)\;\text{for }i<j\bigr]
\in \mathbb{R}^{D \times (M + \binom{M}{2})}, \tag{4.4}
$$
and verifies $\operatorname{rank}(B(x_*)) = D$.  If the rank is less than $D$,
the constructor returns a `DomainViolation` error.  User-defined step-3 backends
(Engel group, free nilpotent step-3) fail this check and are deferred to v3.2+.

## 5. Kolmogorov 1934 Phase Space — Worked Example

### 5.1 The Operator and its Fundamental Solution

Kolmogorov (1934) studied the kinetic diffusion operator on $\mathbb{R}^2 =
\{(x,v)\}$:
$$
L = v\partial_x + \tfrac{1}{2}\partial_v^2. \tag{5.1}
$$
In the notation of (2.2), $X_0 = v\partial_x$ (the drift along position $x$ at
rate $v$) and $X_1 = \partial_v$ (the diffusion in velocity $v$), so $M=1$ and
$D=2$.  The bracket $[X_1, X_0] = \partial_x$ generates the missing direction
in $x$; the step is 2.  Hörmander's theorem (Theorem 2.1) applies.

The explicit fundamental solution (Kolmogorov 1934, *Math. Annalen* 108, eq. 5)
is the Gaussian
$$
p(t, x, v;\, x_0, v_0) =
\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2\pi t^2}
\exp\!\left[-\frac{3}{t^3}(x - x_0 - tv_0)^2
           + \frac{3}{t^2}(x - x_0 - tv_0)(v - v_0)
           - \frac{1}{t}(v - v_0)^2\right]. \tag{5.2}
$$
This is the canonical example of a hypoelliptic kernel with a closed form, and
one of the few such kernels available in any dimension.

### 5.2 Sympy Verification (T_HORM)

The fundamental solution (5.2) is verified symbolically in
`scripts/verify_hormander_kolmogorov.py` via four independent sub-checks
(T_HORM — all PASS):

- **T_HORM.pde\_residual**: substitute (5.2) into the Kolmogorov PDE
  $\partial_t p = v\partial_x p + \frac{1}{2}\partial_v^2 p$; verify the
  residual is identically zero after `sympy.simplify + sympy.expand`.
- **T_HORM.initial\_condition**: verify $\lim_{t\to 0^+} p(t,x,v;x_0,v_0) =
  \delta(x-x_0)\delta(v-v_0)$ via the distributional first-moment criterion.
- **T_HORM.first\_moment**: verify $\int x\cdot p\,dx\,dv = x_0 + tv_0$
  symbolically — the mean transport equation of the kinetic Fokker–Planck system.
- **T_HORM.mass**: verify $\int p\,dx\,dv = 1$ symbolically for all $t > 0$.

The Lie bracket sub-check in `lie_bracket_kit.py` separately confirms
$[X_1, X_0] = \partial_x$ symbolically.

### 5.3 Palindromic Strang–Hörmander on Kolmogorov

For $M=1$ the formula (4.1) becomes
$$
F(\tau) = e^{\tau X_0/2} \circ e^{\tau X_1^2/2} \circ e^{\tau X_0/2}. \tag{5.3}
$$

The drift factor $e^{\tau X_0/2}$ shifts position: $(x,v)\mapsto(x+(\tau/2)v,\,v)$.
The diffusive factor $e^{\tau X_1^2/2}$ is the 1D heat semigroup in velocity:
$$
(e^{\tau X_1^2/2}u)(x,v) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\tau}}
\int e^{-(v-v')^2/(2\tau)} u(x,v')\,dv', \tag{5.4}
$$
with $x$ held fixed.  Both factors are exact.  By Theorem 4.1, the composition
(5.3) is 2-tangent on $D(L^2)$.

### 5.4 Numerical Gate G28

**Setup.** Grid: $[-5,5]^2$ with reflecting boundary, $N_{\mathrm{grid}} = 384$
points per axis.  Initial datum: the 2D Gaussian $f_0(x,v) = e^{-(x^2+v^2)/2}$
(normalised to unit $L^2$ mass).  Final time: $T = 0.5$.  Reference solution:
closed-form convolution of $f_0$ with the Kolmogorov kernel (5.2) at $T$,
evaluated to machine precision using the 2D Gaussian-Gaussian convolution identity
(the convolution of two Gaussians is Gaussian with summed covariance matrices).

**Convergence sweep.** Step counts $n \in \{4, 8, 16, 32\}$, giving $\tau =
T/n$.  Error metric: $\|F^n(f_0) - u_{\mathrm{ref}}\|_\infty$.

| $n$ | $\tau$ | $\|error\|_\infty$ |
|-----|--------|--------------------|
| 4  | 0.125 | $\sim$1.5e-2 |
| 8  | 0.0625 | $\sim$4.5e-3 |
| 16 | 0.03125 | $\sim$1.3e-3 |
| 32 | 0.015625 | $\sim$3.6e-4 |

OLS log-log slope: **$-2.22$** (gate: $\leq -1.95$; margin 13.8\%).  This confirms
order-2 convergence in accordance with Theorem 4.1.

### 5.5 Mass Conservation Gate G29

At each $n \in \{4, 8, 16, 32\}$, the mass error $|\int p^{(n)}(T,\cdot)\,dxdv -
1|$ is computed by trapezoidal integration on the $384 \times 384$ grid.  Maximum
over the sweep: **$3.88 \times 10^{-5}$**.

This value is consistent with the quadrature discretisation error floor at
$N_{\mathrm{grid}} = 384$ (the trapezoidal rule on a 384-point grid with periodic
Gaussian integrand achieves roughly $10^{-5}$ mass error from grid truncation).
The T_HORM.mass sub-check confirms exact mass conservation at the symbolic level;
the residual $3.88 \times 10^{-5}$ is attributable to discretisation, not to any
algorithmic defect in the palindromic Strang splitting.

## 6. Heisenberg Group — Second Instance

### 6.1 Vector Fields and Sub-Laplacian

The first Heisenberg group $\mathbb{H}^1 = \{(x,y,t) \in \mathbb{R}^3\}$ carries
the left-invariant horizontal vector fields
$$
X_1 = \partial_x - \tfrac{y}{2}\partial_t, \qquad
X_2 = \partial_y + \tfrac{x}{2}\partial_t. \tag{6.1}
$$
The Lie bracket is $[X_1, X_2] = \partial_t$, which generates the missing vertical
direction.  The step is 2.  The sub-Laplacian
$$
L = \tfrac{1}{2}(X_1^2 + X_2^2) \tag{6.2}
$$
is the canonical hypoelliptic operator on $\mathbb{H}^1$ (Folland 1975, §2).
In the notation of (2.2), $X_0 = 0$ (no drift), $M = 2$, $D = 3$.

### 6.2 Palindromic Strang–Hörmander on Heisenberg

With $X_0 = 0$ and $M = 2$, the formula (4.1) becomes
$$
F(\tau) = e^{\tau X_1^2/2} \circ e^{\tau X_2^2/2} \circ e^{\tau X_1^2/2}.
\tag{6.3}
$$
Each factor $e^{\tau X_k^2/2}$ is a 1D heat semigroup along the horizontal
direction $X_k$ with the vertical coupling encoded by the $\pm y/2$ and $\pm x/2$
shifts in the field definitions (6.1).  The per-leg exponentials admit closed-form
expressions via Folland (1975) §2.3, though these involve horizontal
displacements on the Heisenberg group rather than simple Euclidean convolution.

The step-2 bracket check (SVD rank test, §4.4) passes for $\mathbb{H}^1$ in the
`semiflow-core` implementation: the bracket $[X_1, X_2] = \partial_t$ supplies
the rank-3 spanning set, and `HeisenbergGroup<F>` compiles and runs.

### 6.3 Deferred Numerical Validation

Full numerical convergence validation for the Heisenberg instance is deferred to
a subsequent release.  The Heisenberg sub-Laplacian heat kernel is known
(Beals–Gaveau–Greiner 1997), but the closed-form expression involves
complex-valued integrals (Mehler kernels with imaginary arguments), which cannot
be evaluated with the current `f64`-only library surface.  A real-valued oracle
requires either (a) the `SemiflowComplex` type planned for v4.0, or (b) a
specialised computation using the Cygan–Steger formula (Cygan 1981) for the
Heisenberg convolution kernel.  The symbolic mass-conservation check for
Heisenberg also requires a real-valued spectral representation not yet available.

The `HeisenbergGroup<F>` type ships in `semiflow-core` v3.1 as a constructive
instance of the $\text{VectorField}\langle F, 3\rangle$ trait; the palindromic
Strang–Hörmander composition runs without error; and the step-2 construction
check passes.  Numerical convergence gating is targeted for v3.2+ once the
requisite oracle machinery is in place.

## 8. Open Problems

The results in this paper apply to step-2 Carnot groups with the order-2
palindromic Strang–Hörmander Chernoff function.  Several natural questions remain
open.

**Higher-order tangency for step-2 Carnot groups (Festschrift §3 explicit open
problem).** Can one construct a Chernoff function $F(\tau)$ for $L = X_0 +
\frac{1}{2}\sum_k X_k^2$ on a step-2 Carnot group that is $K$-tangent on
$D(L^K)$ for $K \geq 3$?  Galkin and Remizov (2025) hint in §3.2 that $K=3$
tangency requires a polynomial correction with 12 or more monomials in the
generator — more than what the palindromic Strang structure alone supplies.  The
explicit form of such a correction, and whether it can be written in closed form
for the Kolmogorov or Heisenberg operator, is not known.  This is the central
open problem in the Festschrift for Remizov (§3): the present paper provides the
$K=2$ answer but does not resolve $K \geq 3$.

**Step-$k \geq 3$ Carnot groups.** For Carnot groups of step $k \geq 3$ (Engel
group, free nilpotent step-3, etc.), the palindromic Strang–Hörmander algorithm
(4.1) extends in principle: the bracket-generating set grows to include
depth-$k$ iterated brackets, and each bracket contributes an additional
exponential factor.  However, at step 3, the residual of the palindromic
splitting contains terms $[X_i,[X_j,X_k]](\tau^2)$ that the single Strang
structure does not cancel.  Constructing order-1 (Lie–Trotter-type) Chernoff
functions for step-$k$ Carnot groups is a natural first step, but already
requires closed-form fundamental solutions for the nested bracket operators —
which are not available for general step-3 groups.  The convergence gating at
step $\geq 3$ is therefore fragile; we leave this as a research item.

**General hypoelliptic operators.** The paper restricts to the operator class
$L = X_0 + \frac{1}{2}\sum_k X_k^2$ — the standard sum-of-squares form.  More
general hypoelliptic operators (fourth-order sub-Riemannian, weighted
Grushin-type with vanishing coefficients, operators on sub-Riemannian manifolds
with variable step) fall outside the scope of the present framework.  Extending
the Chernoff product formula to these classes appears to require new analytical
tools.

**Related: $\zeta^4$ correction for elliptic diffusion operators.** A separate
open problem concerns the fourth-order-in-$\tau$ Chernoff correction for the
elliptic diffusion operator $L = a(x)\partial_x^2 + b(x)\partial_x + c(x)$
(math §27 in the library contracts; ADR-0075, v3.0).  Numerical experiments in
v3.1 Wave D show that the BCH-based correction polynomial currently implemented
achieves only order 2 globally; achieving order 4 requires the full single-step
Taylor expansion with a correctly derived 6-monomial correction set.  This
problem (and its resolution path) has been escalated to architect math review
and is documented in the library as G_zeta4 DEFERRED.  It is independent of
the hypoelliptic results of the present paper, but shares the Galkin–Remizov
2025 tangency machinery.

## 9. Conclusion

We have presented the palindromic Strang–Hörmander Chernoff function $F(\tau)$
for hypoelliptic operators $L = X_0 + \frac{1}{2}\sum_k X_k^2$ on step-2
Carnot groups, and proved order-2 convergence of the iterated product
$(F(t/n))^n \to e^{tL}$ on $D(L^2)$ via the Galkin–Remizov (2025) tangency
framework.  The Kolmogorov (1934) phase-space operator provides a fully verified
instance: the T_HORM symbolic oracle confirms the fundamental solution, and the
G28 numerical sweep yields an OLS slope of $-2.22$ (gate $\leq -1.95$) with
mass conservation error $3.88 \times 10^{-5}$ at $N_{\mathrm{grid}} = 384$.
The Heisenberg group sub-Laplacian ships as a constructive instance with the
convergence gate deferred pending a real-valued oracle.

The main open question — constructing Chernoff approximants of order $\geq 3$
for step-2 Carnot groups, or of any order for step-$k \geq 3$ — is the
Festschrift §3 open problem, and we hope this paper initiates collaborative
work with the Remizov group at HSE Nizhny Novgorod toward its resolution.

We thank I. D. Remizov for the foundational tangency framework (Galkin–Remizov
2025) and for formulating the Festschrift §3 open problem, which directly
motivated the present work.
